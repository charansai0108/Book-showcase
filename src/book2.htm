BOOK: ROMEO AND JULIET

Genre
Tragedy: This book is a classic work of dramatic literature, penned by William Shakespeare. It tells the timeless tale of Romeo and Juliet, two young lovers from feuding families in Verona, Italy. Through its exploration of love, conflict, and fate, this tragedy captures the intensity of human emotion and the consequences of societal division, making it a cornerstone of English literature and a poignant study of passion and loss.

Authors Overview
Written by William Shakespeare, one of the greatest playwrights in history, renowned for his mastery of language and storytelling. Born in 1564 in Stratford-upon-Avon, England, Shakespeare crafted "Romeo and Juliet" around 1595, drawing from earlier Italian tales and his own poetic genius. His works, including this tragedy, have been studied and performed worldwide, cementing his legacy as a literary icon whose insights into human nature remain timeless.
Book Relevance
This book remains profoundly relevant, offering insights into themes of love, conflict, and destiny that resonate across centuries. It examines the destructive power of family feuds, the intensity of youthful passion, and the tragic consequences of miscommunication—issues still pertinent in modern society. With its universal appeal and rich language, "Romeo and Juliet" continues to captivate readers, students, and theatergoers, serving as both a literary masterpiece and a mirror to human experience.
