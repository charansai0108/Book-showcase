BOOK: THE ART OF BEING ALONE

Genre
Self-Help/Psychology: This book falls within the self-help and psychological literature genre, offering a deep dive into the practice and benefits of solitude. It explores how embracing time alone can foster self-discovery, resilience, and inner peace in a world that often prioritizes constant connection. Blending personal anecdotes with research, it provides readers with practical tools to transform loneliness into a powerful, enriching experience.

Authors Overview
Authored by Dr. Elena Carter, a clinical psychologist with over a decade of experience in mindfulness and emotional well-being, and co-authored by Michael Reed, a writer and advocate for intentional living. Dr. Carter, holding a PhD from Stanford University, has published numerous articles on the psychological benefits of solitude, drawing from her work with patients and research studies. Michael Reed brings a personal perspective, having documented his own journey of living alone in essays that resonate with a wide audience. Together, they offer a balanced mix of science and storytelling.
